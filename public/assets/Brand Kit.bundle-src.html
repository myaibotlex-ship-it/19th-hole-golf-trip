§ 05 — Voice & Tone

How we sound

Write like a witty member toasting the table. Short, declarative, evocative — never like a brochure.

✓ Do

  • Short, two-beat phrases. “Tee at dawn. Toast at dusk.”
  • Specific verbs: chase, toast, found, gather, return.
  • Dry humor. Self-deprecating about the score, sincere about the company.
  • Member-first language: “the group,” “the trip,” “our table.”

× Don't

  • Corporate hype: “world-class,” “unparalleled,” “next-level.”
  • Exclamation points or emoji.
  • Aggressive sportiness: “crush it,” “dominate,” “send it.”
  • Over-explaining. If a line needs a footnote, cut it.
